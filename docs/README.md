Road-object detection for autonomous driving faces challenges including dense occlusions, large scale variations, and imaging degradation under nighttime and adverse weather conditions, making it difficult for lightweight detectors to balance accuracy and robustness. To address this, we propose ED-YOLO, a light-weight and robust detection network tailored for autonomous driving. First, we design a Learnable Selective Edge Enhancement Module (LSEM) with channel recalibration to suppress background interference while strengthening discriminative edge cues. Second, we construct a Dual-Path Soft-Sparse Feature Pyramid Network (DSFPN), introducing a detail-compensation path to enhance representations of distant small objects. We further develop a deep Global-Local Soft-Sparse Attention Module (GLSAM) to establish long-range context with low computational overhead and incorporate DySample to mitigate cross-scale align-ment errors. Finally, we propose a Dynamic Task-Aligned Decoupled Head (DTADH), which dynamic task routing and branch alignment alleviate multi-task gradient conflicts, improving classification separability and localization stability under occlusion and inter-class similarity. Experiments show that ED-YOLO achieves 67.0% AP50 and 43.9% AP on BDD-100K. Under a retraining-based evaluation on KITTI, ED-YOLO obtains 93.8% AP50 and 74.5% AP, indicating that the proposed architecture remains effective on another autonomous-driving dataset.
